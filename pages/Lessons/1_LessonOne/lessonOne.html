Common European Framework of Reference:

| Articles

“A” is used before a consonant sound

I have a dog and you have a cat.

You have a cat and I have a parrot.

“An” is used before a vowel sound

I have an apple.

You have an iPhone.

We have an apartment.

| Verb To Be

I "Am"

I am brazilian.

I'm a teacher.

I am not a teacher.

I'm not sad!

Am I your friend?

You, We, They "Are"

You are a doctor.

We are hungry.

We aren't students.

Are they happy?

Are we friends?

He, She, It "Is"

He is a doctor.

She is not hungry.

He isn't a teacher.

Is it true?

Is she famous?
