| Verb To Be

I "Am"

I am brazilian.

I'm a teacher.

I am not a teacher.

I'm not sad!

Am I your friend?

You, We, They "Are"

You are a doctor.

We are hungry.

We aren't students.

Are they happy?

Are we friends?

He, She, It "Is"

He is a doctor.

She is not hungry.

He isn't a teacher.

Is it true?

Is she famous?

| Auxiliaries

- Affirmative

I work as a teacher.

I work in an office.

You work from home.

We like English.

They work at Google.

- Negative

I do not work as a teacher.

You don't work in an office.

We don't like sushi.

They don't work at Google.

- Interrogative

Do you work as a teacher?

Do you work in an office?

Do we like sushi?

Do they work at Google?

| Third Person

Add "S"

He works as a teacher.

She likes to eat sushi.

He loves to drink beer.

She plays cards with her grandma.

Add "Es"

Does he work as a teacher?

She watches X-men 97.

He washes the dishes.

She does not like ice cream.

Add "Ies"

For verbs ending in [Consonant + Y]

He studies economics.

She parties every weekend.

He terrifies her brother.
